# Email Type Rules

## Transactional

Examples: receipt, OTP, password reset, invoice, payment failed, account alert.

- Put clarity and trust first.
- State what happened in the first two lines.
- Avoid hype, promotions, and feature tours.
- Use data panels for references, amounts, dates, or security context.
- Explain the next step or what happens if the user did not request the action.

## Lifecycle

Examples: welcome, onboarding, activation, trial ending, winback.

- Create momentum around one next step.
- Connect the action to immediate product value.
- Do not list every feature.
- Keep the tone human and specific.

## Marketing

Examples: launch, offer, event invite, newsletter.

- Lead with relevance and concrete value.
- Use one primary offer or announcement.
- Avoid fake urgency.
- Keep the layout richer only when it improves comprehension.

## Sales

Examples: demo reminder, follow-up, proposal follow-up.

- Be concise and specific.
- Make the ask low-friction.
- Avoid inflated claims and generic personalization.

## Customer Support

Examples: ticket update, apology, refund, issue resolved.

- Acknowledge the situation plainly.
- State the current status and next step.
- Avoid defensive language.
- Sound accountable and calm.

## Internal Business

Examples: approval request, policy update, team reminder.

- Lead with the action or decision needed.
- Make ownership and timing clear.
- Avoid external marketing tone.
